About Alexa Voice Scramble (AVS)

The Amazon Echo Show is one of Amazon’s “smart devices” in which a user can speak to the device and access its various “skills.” The skills these devices’ can complete are comprehensive, including commands such as “Play the new Rihanna album,” “Find a good cookie recipe,” or “Add grocery shopping to my to-do list.”

To do this, the device records the user’s voice via a built-in microphone before sending these recordings to Amazon’s cloud based audio processing algorithm, Alexa Voice Services (Alexa). While Amazon states that you are able to delete the recording made by your device, it is unclear about what they do with the data from those recordings. If you can’t trust Facebook and Google to not take and use your data despite your clear lack of consent, can you trust Amazon? (Shawdow Progiles)! Alexa uses machine learning to analyze these recordings so the smart device can accurately complete these tasks. Alexa gets more accurate as it receives more popularity and data it gathers increase. (Alexa: Machine Learning)! Every time Alexa misunderstands your command, that data is used to improve the system’s performance the next time around.
